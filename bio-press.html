The Story Behind LaRedoux

Camille Bowie, performing under the moniker LaRedoux (and formerly Redvers), discovered her sanctuary in music during her childhood in Gloucester, England. Seeking refuge from early hardships within the walls of her school's music room, she developed an intimate bond with the piano and songwriting that would shape her entire life. This foundational period ignited a lifelong passion for storytelling through sound, laying the groundwork for an artistic journey that would eventually span decades and cross international borders.

Over the course of her expansive career, Camille performed in more than 20 countries and collaborated with various global ensembles before cultivating her signature solo identity. Today, as LaRedoux, she has mastered the art of live looping. By seamlessly layering acoustic and electric guitars, ambient textures, and raw, distinct vocals, she builds rich, multi-instrumental landscapes entirely on her own, capturing the hearts of a highly interactive global audience online.

Now based in Trois-Rivières, Quebec, LaRedoux continues to push her sonic boundaries, proudly embracing French-language influences alongside her native English. Her extensive discography—featuring vulnerable, human full-length records like If You Want to Fly, You've Got to Let Go and Misty History—reflects a philosophy of radical authenticity. Whether playing major festival stages like FestiVoix or broadcasting cozy, real-time sessions to her dedicated community, her music remains a deeply personal testament to resilience and independent creative freedom.

Press Release

From Gloucester to Quebec: LaRedoux’s Layered Indie-Folk Finds a Grounded Home

TROIS-RIVIÈRES, QC – FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

Indie-folk singer-songwriter LaRedoux (Camille Bowie) is making music that feels entirely human, close to the chest, and free of industry pretense. Originally from Gloucester, England, and having played her way through more than 20 countries over a decades-long musical journey, Camille has spent years quietly sharpening a sound that balances raw songwriting with a completely hands-on approach to performance. Now based in Trois-Rivières, Quebec, she is bringing her deep catalog of indie-folk to local festival stages and an active, tight-knit global community online.

Performing solo, LaRedoux relies on a guitar, a loop pedal, and her instincts to build her tracks piece-by-piece in real time. It is an unhurried, straightforward approach to music-making that strips away the studio fluff, focusing instead on the organic texture of acoustic rhythms, electric accents, and honest vocals. Her songwriting—captured on independent albums like If You Want to Fly, You've Got to Let Go and Misty History—ranges from sharp, everyday observations to deep reflections on life's transitions. Whether she is running an interactive, request-driven livestream or taking the stage at regional staples like the FestiVoix de Trois-Rivières, LaRedoux keeps the focus exactly where it belongs: on the songs, the stories, and a genuine connection with the room.
